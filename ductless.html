DUCTLESS MINI SPLIT SYSTEMS

Add promo (if applicable) for ductless systems

Traditional systems like central air conditioners and furnaces may not always be ideal for every home, especially in older buildings where installing ductwork can be challenging or impossible. Ductless heating and cooling systems, however, don't require ductwork, making them a flexible solution that can be installed in almost any type of building or room.


ADVANTAGES OF A DUCTLESS SYSTEM

  • No Need for ductwork: This is ideal for homes without existing ducts, such as older buildings or homes with limited space for traditional systems.

  • Energy Efficiency:Ductless HVAC systems are more energy-efficient since they avoid the energy loss that typically occurs in ducts.

  • Easy installation: Since ductless systems don't require ducts to be installed, the setup process is quicker and less invasive compared to traditional systems.

  • Zone Control:Ductless systems allow you to control the temperature in different areas or "zones" of your home independently, offering customized comfort and reducing energy consumption in unused spaces.

WHERE CAN I INSTALL A DUCTLESS SYSTEM?

A ductless HVAC system is versatile and can be installed in a variety of locations. Here are some common areas where you can install a ductless system:


  • Living Rooms
  • Bedrooms
  • Kitchens
  • Bathrooms
  • Baby Nurseries
  • Attics
  • Garages & more!

TYPES OF DUCTLESS HVAC SYSTEMS

  • 1. Ductless Mini-Split Systems: This is a type of air conditioning and heating system that consists of two main parts: an indoor unit (the part you see inside your room) and an outdoor unit (the compressor), which are connected by a small refrigerant line. The indoor unit can be mounted on the wall, or in some cases, installed on the floor or ceiling.
  • 2. Ductless Multi-Split Systems:This system is similar to a mini-split but can connect multiple indoor units to just one outdoor unit. Each indoor unit can control the temperature in different rooms.
  • 2. Ductless Mini-Split with Ceiling Cassette:: This system has the indoor unit hidden in the ceiling, making it almost invisible. It blows air out in all directions to cool or heat the room.
  • 4. Ductless Floor-Mounted Systems:Instead of being on the wall or ceiling, the indoor unit sits on the floor. It works just like the other ductless systems, but itâ€™s great for spaces with limited wall or ceiling room.
  • 5. Ductless Hybrid Systems:This system combines the benefits of ductless technology with traditional heating and cooling systems. It can use your existing ducts alongside a ductless system for better airflow and efficiency.
